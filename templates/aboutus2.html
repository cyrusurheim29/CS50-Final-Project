About This Project

Equal Opportunity Boston is an initiative towards a more socially just future. Volunteering represents our everyday efforts at forging interpersonal bonds and interweaving different parts of the communities around us. The work of people in the counties, cities, boroughs, neighborhoods that compose our communities have made the Boston Metro Area the city with the highest informal helping rate in the country. This work is unpaid and largely unseen, but it makes all the difference. Our goal is to integrate the databases of volunteer opportunities in Boston's boroughs with socioeconomic trends in housing, food, and health disparity that give these regions contour and dictate the urgency of their need. Boroughs such as Fenway/Kenmore, Back Bay, and Cambridge benefit immensely from college student volunteers in proximal universities. However, this density of college students leaves many more regions underserved. Students deserve the tools to make well-informed decisions on how to help, and, more importantly, communities deserve Equal Opportunity to receive means to grow.
